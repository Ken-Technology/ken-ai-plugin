## Elevator Pitch

A few-second hook the CSM could say out loud to the primary ICP if they bumped into them in an elevator. Goes at the very top of `research.md`.

**Format:**
```
> If you met {primary ICP - role at company type} in an elevator with {Company Name}, what should you say in a couple seconds to make them really hooked?

{1-3 sentence answer}
```

**Rules:**
- Name the ICP specifically in the question (e.g., "VP of Sales at a 200-person B2B SaaS"). Pull from the Target Market section - don't invent.
- Answer in 1-3 sentences, spoken-English, no buzzwords. If you wouldn't say it out loud, rewrite.
- Lead with the sharpest pain or outcome for that ICP, then the mechanism. End with curiosity, not a CTA.
- Extract from sources only. If the sources don't support a clear pitch, write a conservative version grounded in the product's actual mechanism.

**Bad**: "We're an AI-powered platform that helps companies scale outbound."
**Good**: "Most heads of sales I talk to are burning a full SDR salary just to keep a list clean - we replace that with a pipeline that finds and verifies leads overnight, so your reps wake up to a queue instead of a spreadsheet."

## Executive Summary

Write a comprehensive executive summary in 3 paragraphs for high-level industry and category understanding.

**Paragraph 1 - Business Overview**: What they do, who they serve, their market position, and core value proposition.

**Paragraph 2 - Differentiation & Approach**: Key differentiators, unique approach, how they win leads and deals, competitive advantages.

**Paragraph 3 - Strategic Context**: Target market insights, proven results, strategic priorities, key opportunities for outbound success.

Every sentence should provide strategic value. Be concise but rich in detail.

## Product Overview

### What it is
Write 2-3 paragraphs explaining:
- Core functionality and how it actually works
- Key architecture/technical approach that makes it different
- The fundamental approach or methodology

**Critical**: Never say "innovative," "cutting-edge," or generic terms. Explain the actual mechanism.

Bad: "AI-powered solution"
Good: "Uses ML models trained on 50M data points to predict churn 14 days before it happens"

### Benefits & Features
Focus on what makes them UNIQUE compared to competitors. Maximum 3 focused paragraphs covering:
- Core unique capabilities (what they do that others don't)
- Distinctive features that solve real problems
- Performance/deployment advantages that matter

For each feature, explain the actual pain point it solves and specific value provided.

### Problems it solves
List 3-5 key problems with this structure:

```
**[Problem Title]**

- **The Pain**: 2-3 sentences explaining the specific business pain. Include quantifiable impact (e.g., "costs teams 15 hours/week," "results in 30% churn").
- **The Solution**: How their product solves this (be specific about the mechanism)
- **Outbound Angle**: Exact question or hook for cold outreach (e.g., "How much time does your team waste manually reconciling data across 5+ tools?")
```

### Why this matters
Write 2-3 paragraphs covering:
- Market context and why this problem is urgent NOW
- Business/financial impact of not solving this
- Who benefits and what changes for them

Focus on ROI, business metrics, or operational impact.

### Pricing
Include if available:
- Pricing tiers with starting prices
- What's included in each tier
- Current ACV (Average Contract Value)
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) if mentioned
- Typical sales cycle length

### Unique Competitive Advantages
List 3-5 advantages that are their "secret sauce" - things competitors can't easily replicate:

```
**[Advantage Title]**

- **What Makes This Different**: 2-3 sentences on the specialized capability, proprietary approach, or unique positioning. Explain WHY this is hard to replicate.
- **Outbound Angle**: How to position this in sales conversations to create urgency
```

## Case Studies

**If no case studies found**: Output "No case studies found in provided resources." and stop.

Select ONLY the 3 best case studies matching primary ICP with strong, measurable results.

For each:

```
### [Customer Name]

**Customer Type**: [Industry, ICP type, company size]

**Situation**: [2-3 sentences on business context and challenge before using product]

**Results Achieved**:
Focus on specific, measurable outcomes with hard numbers:
- Revenue impact (e.g., "Increased revenue by $2M")
- Efficiency gains (e.g., "Reduced processing time from 5 hours to 30 minutes")
- Cost savings (e.g., "Cut operational costs by $500K annually")
- Performance metrics (e.g., "Improved conversion from 2% to 8%")
- Time savings (e.g., "Saved 20 hours per week")

**Competitive Context** (if available): [How this compares to alternatives]

**GTM Application**: [When to use in outreach - which ICP, funnel stage, messaging angle]
```

Prioritize case studies with hard numbers over qualitative outcomes.

## Lead Magnets

Document ONLY existing lead magnets - do not suggest new ones.

Lead magnets include: Free tools, assessments, audits, consultations, trials, demos, educational content (PDFs, videos, webinars, guides), templates, ROI calculators, communities.

Select the 3 best existing lead magnets most likely to drive meeting bookings.

For each:

```
### [Lead Magnet Name/Title]

**Type/Format**: [e.g., Free Tool, PDF Guide, ROI Calculator, Free Trial, Audit, Webinar]

**What It Is**: [2-3 sentences describing what it contains/provides]

**What It Does**: [Value provided and problem solved for prospect]

**Strength Assessment**: [Strong/Moderate/Weak + explanation]
- Strong = high perceived value, easy to consume, naturally leads to sales
- Weak = generic, low perceived value, doesn't advance sales process

**Access Details**: [Link if available, otherwise describe how to access]

**Outbound Usage Strategy**:
- Best for: [Email 1, 2, 3, etc.]
- ICP segments: [Which audiences]
- Messaging angle: [How to present it]
```

## Additional Context

Document valuable information from resources not covered in previous sections. Only include what EXISTS in resources - never generate or suggest.

Maximum 1 short paragraph per point. Possible topics:

**Past Outbound Efforts**: What worked, what didn't, key learnings

**Sales Process**: Cycle length, velocity, conversion rates, funnel stages

**Current Marketing Channels**: Paid ads, SEO, content marketing performance

**Decision-Maker Dynamics**: Buying committee, approval process, influencers vs buyers

**Pain Point Language**: Specific phrases or metaphors that resonate

**Onboarding Model**: Customer success approach, aha moments, migration services

**Strategic Context**: Growth targets, timeline, funding, recent changes

**Competitive Landscape**: Key competitors, positioning, win/loss patterns

If no additional context beyond covered sections: "No additional context beyond information covered in previous sections."
