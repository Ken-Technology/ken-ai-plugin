## Extra Info
### Exercises
_If you actually do this, it will turn your copy from good to great._

1. **Word Reduction Exercise**
    *   Try to strip 50% of the words (while maintaining sense)
    *   You might not hit 50%, but you'll get better, tighter copy
2. **Read Aloud Test**
    *   Read the entire copy out loud
    *   Identifies awkward phrasing immediately
3. **Write Without Adjectives/Adverbs**
    *   Challenge yourself to create compelling copy using only nouns and verbs
    *   Forces stronger word choices
4. **Objection Handling**
    *   List top 5 reasons someone might say "no"
    *   Write 1-2 sentences directly addressing each objection within the copy
5. **"So What?" Test**
    *   Take every line and ask "So what?"
    *   If you can't justify it, cut it
6. **Feature-to-Story Transformation**
    *   Pick a "boring" feature (e.g., "24/7 customer support")
    *   Rewrite as mini-story: "At 2AM, your system crashes. You're panicked. Within 3 minutes, our engineer is on the line..."

### Tips
#### Writing Techniques
*   Use active voice instead of passive voice
*   Explain complex products/services using only common, everyday language
*   Write entire emails using sentences of 15 words or fewer
*   Hierarchy: Pain points > benefits > features
*   Start sentences with "You" more often than "I" or "We"
*   Use concrete numbers instead of vague terms ("14 days" not "two weeks")
*   Replace jargon with simple explanations
*   Write at 8th-grade reading level for maximum comprehension
*   Use contractions (it's, you'll) to sound more natural
*   Include specific timeframes ("3 minutes" vs "quickly")

#### Advanced Copy Techniques
*   **Pattern Interrupts:** Break expectations to maintain attention
*   **Future Pacing:** Help them visualize success after using your solution
*   **Micro-Commitments:** Get small yeses before the big ask
*   **Trojan Horse:** Lead with something they want, deliver what they need
*   **The Damaging Admission:** Admit a small flaw to build trust
*   **Specificity Sells:** "2,847 companies" beats "thousands of companies"
*   **Social Proof Stacking:** Layer different types of proof (numbers + names + testimonials)

#### Psychological Triggers
*   **Loss Aversion:** What they'll miss out on
*   **Social Proof:** Others are already doing this
*   **Authority:** Credentials and expertise
*   **Reciprocity:** Give value first
*   **Scarcity:** Limited availability or time
*   **Curiosity Gaps:** Tease information they want
*   **Identity:** Align with who they want to be

#### Power Words
Use power words to boost conversions. You can ask AI to rewrite your copy using power words.

### What NOT to Do
Don't follow templates blindly. Most cold email templates online range from horrible to average - they sound like obvious cold emails and get ignored.

#### The Offensive Words Rule
**Never use offensive words.** This is both a spam filter issue and a professionalism issue.

**Important distinction:**
*   Don't use offensive words does not mean don't offend people
*   You can be polite while being controversial
*   You can have controversial opinions that are still agreeable
*   Be controversial but professional

**The balance:**
*   Controversial but agreeable opinions - good
*   Challenging conventional thinking politely - good
*   Cursing or crude language - bad
*   Personal attacks or insults - bad
*   Discriminatory language - bad

**Key principle:** You should be controversial enough to stand out, but agreeable enough that 80-90% of your audience won't be turned off.

**Opening Mistakes:**
*   Starting with "I hope this email finds you well"
*   "Sorry to bother you"
*   "I know you're busy, but..."
*   "Just following up"
*   Company introductions in first line
*   Weather, restaurant, school, soccer, or holiday references

**Body Mistakes:**
*   Walls of text (paragraphs > 3 lines)
*   Multiple ideas in one email
*   Feature dumps without benefits
*   Generic value props that apply to everyone
*   Trying to educate before engaging
*   Name-dropping without context
*   False urgency or fake deadlines
*   Obviously templated sections

**CTA Mistakes:**
*   Multiple calls to action
*   Vague CTAs ("Let me know your thoughts")
*   High-commitment first asks ("Can we schedule a 1-hour demo?")
*   Calendar links in first email
*   Assumptive CTAs ("When can we meet?")

**Tone Mistakes:**
*   Being too formal/corporate
*   Excessive exclamation points!!!
*   ALL CAPS for emphasis
*   Too many emojis
*   Desperate language ("Please, please reply")
*   Arrogant or condescending tone
*   Being too casual/unprofessional

**Technical Mistakes:**
*   Broken personalization tags {FirstName}
*   Multiple links
*   Attachments in cold emails
*   Images that don't load
*   Signatures longer than email body
*   Colored text or fancy fonts
*   Email longer than 150 words (first touch)

**Strategic Mistakes:**
*   Sending to generic emails (info@, support@)
*   Not researching the company
*   No follow-up sequence planned
*   Same message to different ICPs
*   Pitching competitors' clients with same message
