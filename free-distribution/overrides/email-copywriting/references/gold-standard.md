# The Gold Standard - What 9-10/10 Copy Looks Like

Two operator-written campaigns (Cristian, Ken AI campaigns 431 and 2157) are the quality bar for every sequence this skill produces. Study them before drafting. The default AI draft is a 6-7/10; the difference between that and these is not talent, it is a set of concrete habits listed below.

```
Cristian Frunze

```

## Exemplar 2: One-sentence product pitch (campaign 2157, "Ken Daily")

**Entire email (static body ~41 words):**

```
Hey {firstName},

{{First Line}}

I created a newsletter that emails you 10 qualified leads that match your ICP every morning, and it also includes a personalized email you can send to them.

You don't need to pay anything, just sign up if you want it:
https://ken.so/daily

Best,
{sender_first_name} - Ken AI
```

The A/B here tested **which product angle leads** (daily leads first vs full campaign first) - again the offer, not adjectives.

## The patterns that make these 9-10/10

1. **The offer fits in one breath.** One plain sentence carries the whole deal: concrete deliverable + who it's for + what they get. "We'll send 3,000 personalized emails to your ideal clients on us." / "A newsletter that emails you 10 qualified leads matching your ICP every morning." If you cannot state the client's offer this way, you are not ready to draft - go back to the research and find the sentence first.
2. **Concrete nouns and numbers do all the selling.** 3,000 emails, 10 leads, every morning, 7 meetings per 10k, 40+ companies. The only adjectives are information-bearing ("qualified", "personalized", "ideal"). Zero hype adjectives, zero abstract benefit language ("streamline", "supercharge", "transform" appear nowhere).
3. **Mechanism in verbs, not concepts.** "We write the copy, build the list, handle everything." Three verbs replace a paragraph of "end-to-end done-for-you outbound solution".
4. **Risk reversal is shown, not claimed.** The word "guarantee" barely appears; instead there is a bottle of TOST Rose and a mini pinata. A specific, physical, slightly absurd consequence is more believable than any percent-certainty claim - and it is unmistakably human.
5. **Kill the top objections in one line, without spam words.** "You don't need to pay anything, just sign up if you want it" removes cost and commitment in 13 words - no "free", no "no obligation".
6. **Micro-CTAs.** "Want to try it?" - "Worth a toast?" - "just reply." Five words or fewer, answerable with one word.
7. **Value math makes the offer tangible.** The one long email in the sequence is a numbered value stack where every line carries a number and a worth. Prose never gets that budget; lists of real numbers do.
8. **One flourish of personality per email, max.** The toast joke, the pinata, "You can even send pics." One human beat per email - not a comedy routine, and never a pun-filled AI attempt at "casual".
9. **Callbacks stitch the sequence.** "Worth a toast?" in Email 2 only works because of Email 1. Write the sequence as one conversation, not three isolated emails.
10. **Follow-ups shrink.** ~72 words, then the value stack, then ~35-45 word closes. The final email is belief ("I really believe we can help {company}... otherwise I wouldn't make such a crazy offer") or honest scarcity ("each one takes real time from our team") - never "just following up".
11. **Test the offer variable, not the adjective.** Every variant changes something a prospect would describe differently to a colleague (the metric, the leading angle, the guarantee). If both variants would be described the same way over coffee, the test is worthless.
12. **Bare `{firstName},` is a valid follow-up greeting.** Email 1 opens `Hey {firstName},`; follow-ups in the same thread may open with just `{firstName},` - it reads like a real person continuing a conversation.

## The compression test (run on every draft)

After drafting Email 1, ask:

- Could the prospect repeat the offer to a colleague after one skim? If they'd say "some AI outbound thing", rewrite until they'd say "they send 3,000 personalized emails to my ICP and I don't pay unless it works".
- Delete every sentence and check what breaks. Any sentence whose removal changes nothing is already dead - remove it.
- Is there a sentence that only exists to sound smart? (Industry observations, "in today's landscape", restating their pain back at them.) Cut it.
- Does every number survive the "would I say this number out loud to their face" test?

A 9/10 email is not a shorter 6/10 email. It is a better offer, stated plainly.
