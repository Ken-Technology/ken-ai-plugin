# The Building Block Approach

## Philosophy

Treat copy as building blocks. Combine these building blocks so that, in the end, something nice flows. When you treat copy like building blocks - like Legos - you can test as many combinations as possible, and it becomes relatively easy to iterate.

The approach is to test as many combinations as possible to see which one works. It's about sheer volume of combinations. For us, volume is how we discover what works and then double down on it.

**Key Principle:**

> It's not about creating one perfect piece of copy and sending it to everyone. It's about creating many different combinations of copy and sending the right combinations to specific avatars within our audience.

## How This Applies to Campaign Strategy

When creating campaigns, think in terms of testable building blocks:

**ICP Blocks:**
- Different company sizes
- Different industries
- Different roles/titles

**Messaging Angle Blocks:**
- Pain point focused
- Opportunity focused
- Social proof focused
- ROI/results focused

**Offer Blocks:**
- Demo/meeting request
- Free resource/lead magnet
- Case study share
- Quick question/reply

**Sequence Structure Blocks:**
- 3-email quick touch
- 5-email nurture
- 4-email with lead magnet

## Building Block Testing Strategy

1. **Start with hypotheses** - What combinations do you think will work?
2. **Create variations** - Build different combinations of blocks
3. **Test in parallel** - Run multiple campaigns simultaneously
4. **Measure and learn** - Which combinations perform best?
5. **Double down** - Scale what works, cut what doesn't
