## Strategy
_How to structure campaigns_
### The Building Block Approach
You should treat copy as building blocks. Combine these building blocks so that, in the end, something nice flows. When you treat copy like building blocks - like Legos - you can test as many combinations as possible, and it becomes relatively easy to iterate.

The approach is to test as many combinations as possible to see which one works. It's about sheer volume of combinations. For us, volume is how we discover what works and then double down on it.

**Key principle:** It's not about creating one perfect piece of copy and sending it to everyone. It's about creating many different combinations of copy and sending the right combinations to specific avatars within our audience

### Integration with Campaign Strategy
When a Campaign Strategy document is provided, the strategy work is already done. Your job is to:
1. Follow the email sequence blueprint exactly
2. Write copy that fulfills each email's `primary_goal`
3. Honor the `description` guidance for each email
4. Insert AI variables where specified
5. Generate the corresponding prompts.json

The Campaign Strategy defines:
- Value propositions to emphasize
- ICP characteristics and pain points
- CTA approach (reply vs. click)
- Lead magnet integration points
- AI personalization variables

Your email copy is the **execution layer** of that strategy.
